Places I've skiied

Crystal Mountain

Crystal has a wonderful lodge at the summit where you can get great food at a sit-down restaurant. There are two other lodges on the mountain too.

One way to get to the summit is via a gondola ride that is comfortable and has great views.

I've gone to Crystal twice now. There's a wide diversity in runs for every skill level. My favorite run is the Blue at the summit, but I forget what it's called.

There's also places to stay right on the mountain as well as a short drive from the mountain.

Snoqualmie

If you live near Highland Middle School, Snoqualmie is a great option for skiing because it is so close.

I go here all the time. Last winter, I skiied here nearly every weekend.

There are multiple places to eat, but my favorite is the BBQ that serves chili and beef brisket. Yum!

My favorite lift is Silver Fur because it takes you to Hog Wild and the Silver Fur runs which have a lot of jumps.

The runs vary in skill level, but there's not as many runs as Crystal.

Whistler Mountain

Whistler is the fanciest ski resort I've been to. We stayed at the Fairmon and you could ski right from the hotel to the lift and back.

Whistler is located near Vancouver, Canada. So you have to have a passport to go there.

There's a whole alpine village at the base of the mountain with a ton of hotels and restaurants.

Mount Hood

This is the only place in the US that has year round skiing. I went skiing with my dad and friend this past summer.

In the summer, you can go skiing, and then paddle boarding in a lake, in the same day! And that's what we did when we went.
